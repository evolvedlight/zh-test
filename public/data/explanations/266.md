### The Core Definition
The correct answer is **(b)**: The maximum extent of Switzerland from North to South is approximately **220 kilometers**.

### Compact Dimensions
Switzerland is a relatively small country.
*   **Northernmost Point:** Bargen in the Canton of Schaffhausen.
*   **Southernmost Point:** Chiasso in the Canton of Ticino.
*   **Travel Time:** Despite the short distance, traveling from north to south takes several hours because you have to cross the **Alps**.
*   **The Gotthard Axis:** Most traffic (and trains) follows the Gotthard route to bridge this north-south divide.

### Comparison
To put this in perspective, 220 km is less than the distance between London and Liverpool. It shows how concentrated Swiss geography is, containing diverse language regions and mountain landscapes in a very compact area.
