### The Core Definition
The correct answer is **(a)**: Political parties function as mediators that **represent various interests and concerns of the population**.

### Key Functions
1.  **Recruitment:** They find and train candidates for political office (Parliament, Government).
2.  **Information:** They provide opinions on referendums and initiatives (voting recommendations), helping citizens form their own views.
3.  **Aggregation:** They take many individual opinions and combine them into a coherent political program.
4.  **Debate:** They provide a forum for discussing the country's future.

### What they do NOT do:
*   **Elect the Government (b):** Technically, the *Parliament* (the individual members) elects the government, though parties organize the strategy.
*   **Control Media (c):** In a democracy, the media must be independent of parties.
*   **Mediate Office Disputes (d):** This is handled by administrative law and internal government hierarchy.
