### The Core Definition
The correct answer is **(b)**: The traditional card game of Switzerland is called **Jass**.

### A Swiss Passion
Jassing is more than just a game; it is a cultural institution.
*   **Deck:** There are two main types of decks used: the **French** deck (Hearts, Diamonds, Spades, Clubs) in Western Switzerland and the **Swiss-German** deck (Roses, Bells, Acorns, Shields) in Central and Eastern Switzerland (including Zurich).
*   **Variants:** The most common version is the "Schieber" for four players in two teams.
*   **Where to play:** You can find people jassing in almost every traditional Swiss pub (Stammtisch), at family gatherings, and even in national TV tournaments.

### Culture
Jass bridges generations and social classes. It is common for high-ranking politicians or CEOs to enjoy a game of Jass with farmers or workers. Knowing how to "Jass" is often considered a sign of being well-integrated into Swiss social life.
