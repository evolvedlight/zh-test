### The Core Definition
The correct answer is **(d)**: Every woman who works and has a child has a **legal right to maternity leave** (Mutterschaftsurlaub).

### The Rules
*   **Duration:** The federal law guarantees **14 weeks** (98 days) of leave. Some companies or cantonal laws (like in Geneva) offer more.
*   **Payment:** During this time, the woman receives **80% of her salary** (up to a maximum daily amount), paid through the "Erwerbsersatzordnung" (EO).
*   **Eligibility:** To get the pay, the woman must have been insured with the AHV for at least 9 months and have worked for at least 5 months during the pregnancy.
*   **Protection:** A woman cannot be fired during her pregnancy or in the 16 weeks following the birth.

### Paternity Leave
Since 2021, fathers also have a legal right to **2 weeks** of paid paternity leave (Vaterschaftsurlaub), which can be taken within the first 6 months after the child is born.
