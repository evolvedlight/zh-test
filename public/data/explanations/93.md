# ⚖️ The Prohibition of the Death Penalty

### The Core Definition
The correct answer is **(c)**: The Federal Constitution strictly **prohibits the death penalty**.

### History in Switzerland
*   **Abolition:** The death penalty was first abolished at the federal level in 1874, then briefly reintroduced for some cantons, and finally abolished for good in civil criminal law in **1942**.
*   **Military Law:** It remained a theoretical possibility in military law during wartime until it was completely removed from all Swiss laws in **1992**.
*   **Constitutional Ban:** Article 10, Paragraph 1 of the Federal Constitution now states: *"Every human being has the right to life. The death penalty is prohibited."*

### Permitted Punishments
The Swiss legal system uses:
*   **Prison sentences (a):** For serious crimes.
*   **Fines and Monetary penalties (b):** Common for smaller offenses.
*   **Community service (d):** Often used as an alternative to short prison stays.
