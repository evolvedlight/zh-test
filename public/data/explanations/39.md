### The Core Definition
The correct answer is **(b)**: The Federal Palace (Bundeshaus) in Bern is the seat of the **Federal Assembly (Parliament)** and the **Federal Council (Government)**.

### A Symbol of Unity
Built in various stages between 1852 and 1902, the Bundeshaus is the political heart of Switzerland.
*   **The Dome:** Features the coats of arms of the 22 cantons (as they existed in 1902) in stained glass.
*   **The Chambers:** Contains the meeting rooms for the National Council and the Council of States.
*   **The Federal Council Wing:** Where the seven members of the government have their offices and hold their weekly meetings.

### Fun Fact
The square in front of the Bundeshaus (Bundesplatz) is a popular meeting place with a water feature representing the 26 cantons. The building is open to the public for guided tours when parliament is not in session.
