### The Core Definition
The correct answer is **(c)**: Employees and self-employed persons with children are entitled to **family allowances (Familienzulagen)**.

### What are they?
They are monthly payments intended to partially offset the costs of raising children.
1.  **Child Allowance (Kinderzulage):** Paid for children from birth until age 16 (at least 200 CHF per month, though Zurich pays more).
2.  **Education Allowance (Ausbildungszulage):** Paid for children aged 16 to 25 who are still in education/training (at least 250 CHF per month).

### How to get them?
*   **Employees:** Usually apply through their employer.
*   **Self-employed:** Apply through their social security office (AHV-Ausgleichskasse).
*   **Financing:** These allowances are funded by employers (and some self-employed contributions).
