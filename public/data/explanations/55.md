### The Core Definition
The correct answer is **(c)**: The Swiss national flag is a **white cross on a red background**.

### Distinctive Features
*   **The Shape:** Unlike most national flags which are rectangular, the Swiss flag is **square**. The only exception is the Swiss ensign used by ships at sea, which is rectangular.
*   **The Ratio:** The arms of the cross are 1/6 longer than they are wide.

### History
*   **Battle of Laupen (1339):** Swiss soldiers first wore white crosses on their clothing to recognize each other.
*   **1848:** The white cross on a red field became the official flag of the newly formed federal state.
*   **The Red Cross:** The international humanitarian organization "Red Cross" (founded in Geneva) reversed the Swiss flag (red cross on white) to honor Switzerland and its neutrality.

### Symbolism
The flag represents freedom, honor, and the unity of the cantons. It is a source of great pride and is frequently seen on public buildings, mountain peaks, and chocolate packaging!
