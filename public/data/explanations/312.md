### The Core Definition
The correct answer is **(b)**: If the emergency sirens wail in a real emergency, you should **listen to the radio**.

### General Alarm
Switzerland has a nationwide network of around 5,000 stationary and 2,200 mobile sirens.
*   **The Sound:** A steady oscillating tone lasting one minute (General Alarm).
*   **Action:** When you hear it, the official instruction is:
    1.  Listen to the **radio** (SRF or local stations) or check the **Alertswiss app**.
    2.  Follow the instructions of the authorities.
    3.  Inform your neighbors.
*   **Water Alarm:** A series of short, 20-second tones means there is a danger from a dam breach. In this case, you must leave the endangered area immediately.

### Annual Test
Don't panic if you hear sirens on the **first Wednesday of February**! That is the annual national test day, and it is announced in advance in all media.
