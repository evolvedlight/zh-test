### The Core Definition
The correct answer is **(c)**: The coat of arms (Wappen) of the Canton and City of Zurich is a **shield divided diagonally from top left to bottom right into blue and white**.

### Design and History
*   **The Colors:** Blue and white have been the colors of Zurich since the 14th century.
*   **The Division:** The division is "per bend" (diagonal). In the heraldry of the flag, the blue part is at the bottom (closer to the flagpole) and the white part is at the top.
*   **Origin:** The colors likely originate from the blue of the Lake Zurich and the white of the linen industry, or from the banner of the Zurich military in the Middle Ages.

### Usage
You will see this shield on:
*   Official government buildings and documents.
*   License plates of cars registered in the canton (ZH).
*   Public transport vehicles and city signage.
*   The flag of Zurich, which is square and follows the same color pattern.
