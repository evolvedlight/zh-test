### The Core Definition
The correct answer is **(a)**: You can find information about local regulations and municipal offers in **local newspapers or on the internet**.

### Official Sources
Municipalities have a legal duty to inform their residents.
*   **Official Publication (Amtliches Publikationsorgan):** Every municipality has a designated newspaper or website where new laws, building permits, and election results *must* be published to be valid.
*   **Websites:** Most Zurich municipalities (e.g., www.stadt-zuerich.ch or www.winterthur.ch) have excellent websites where you can find everything from trash collection dates to voting results.
*   **Brochures:** New residents often receive a "Willkommensbrosch√ºre" (welcome brochure) with all the most important local contacts and rules.

### Why not TV?
National TV (b) covers big-picture Swiss or international news. It would be impossible for them to report on a small village's new zoning law or a neighborhood park opening. For local issues, the "Lokalzeitung" (like the "Tages-Anzeiger" or smaller local papers) is the best source.
