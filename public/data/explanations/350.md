# ✍️ Gottfried Keller: The Zurich Writer

### The Core Definition
The correct answer is **(b)**: Gottfried Keller (1819–1890) was a famous Zurich **writer** (Schriftsteller).

### A National Literary Giant
Keller is one of the most important German-language authors of the 19th century.
*   **Works:** He is best known for his novel *Green Henry* (Der grüne Heinrich) and his collection of short stories *The People of Seldwyla* (Die Leute von Seldwyla), which include the famous story "Romeo and Juliet in the Village."
*   **Political Role:** Keller was not just a poet; he was also politically active and served as the **First Secretary of the Canton of Zurich** (Staatsschreiber) for many years.
*   **Style:** His writing is characterized by its realistic descriptions of Swiss life, its humor, and its deep understanding of human character and social changes.

### Legacy
Keller was a passionate defender of the young Swiss federal state. Today, his portrait appears on medals and he is celebrated as the "poet of Zurich." His house and various monuments in the city remind us of his lasting influence on Swiss literature and identity.
