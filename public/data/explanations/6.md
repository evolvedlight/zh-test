*   If they succeed, a national vote (referendum) is held.

### Optional vs. Mandatory
*   **Optional Referendum:** For normal laws. Requires signatures.
*   **Mandatory Referendum:** Required automatically if the Parliament wants to change the **Constitution** or join a supranational community.

### Modern Relevance
Recent examples include referendums on the CO2 Act or the Covid-19 Act. It forces politicians to compromise *before* passing laws, knowing the people can have the final say.
