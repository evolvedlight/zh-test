### The Core Definition
The correct answer is **(d)**: In Roman times, the name of the settlement that became Zurich was **Turicum**.

### A Roman Customs Post
The Romans established Turicum around 15 BC on the **Lindenhof** hill.
*   **Location:** It was strategically located where the Limmat river flows out of Lake Zurich.
*   **Purpose:** Turicum was primarily a **customs station** (Statio Turicensis) where taxes were collected on goods being transported along the waterway.
*   **Ruins:** Remains of Roman baths, walls, and a tombstone with the name "Turicum" have been found in the old town.

### Other Roman Names
*   **Vitudurum (a):** The Roman name for **Winterthur**.
*   **Vindonissa (b):** The Roman legionary camp in **Windisch** (Aargau).
*   **Aquae Helveticae (c):** The Roman name for the spa town of **Baden**.
