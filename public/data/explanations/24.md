### The Core Definition
The correct answer is **(d)**: The basic law of the Swiss Confederation is called the **Bundesverfassung (Federal Constitution)**.

### What it Contains
*   **Structure:** It defines how the state is organized (Federal Council, Parliament, Courts).
*   **Tasks:** It lists what the Confederation can do (e.g., currency, foreign policy, military).
*   **Rights:** It guarantees fundamental rights (e.g., freedom of opinion, religion, press).

### History
*   **1848:** First Constitution (created modern Switzerland).
*   **1874:** Total revision (added direct democracy like Referendum).
*   **1999:** Total revision (modernized language and structure).
*   **Continuous Change:** Unlike the US Constitution, the Swiss one is changed frequently (several times a year) by **Popular Initiatives** and **Mandatory Referendums**.
