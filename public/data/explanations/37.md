### The Core Definition
The correct answer is **(b)**: The central task of the Federal Assembly (Parliament) is to **deliberate on and pass laws**.

### The Legislative Process
The Parliament is the **Legislative branch**. Its main duties include:
1.  **Law-making:** Creating new federal laws and amending existing ones.
2.  **Budgeting:** Approving the state's spending and income.
3.  **Elections:** Electing the Federal Council, the Federal Chancellor, and the Federal Judges.
4.  **Oversight:** Supervising the Federal Council and the Federal Administration to ensure they follow the law.

### What it does NOT do:
*   **Organizing Elections (a):** This is the job of the Federal Chancellery and the Cantons.
*   **Government Business (c):** Executing laws and running the country is the job of the **Federal Council** (Executive).
*   **Court Rulings (d):** This is the job of the **Federal Supreme Court** (Judiciary).
