### The Core Definition
The correct answer is **(b)**: **Elisabeth Kopp** was the first woman elected to the Swiss Federal Council.

### A Historic Moment
*   **Election:** Elisabeth Kopp (FDP, Zurich) was elected by the Federal Parliament in **1984**, thirteen years after women received the right to vote at the federal level.
*   **Department:** She led the **Federal Department of Justice and Police**.
*   **Significance:** Her election was a breakthrough for gender equality in Switzerland. Since then, many other women have served in the government (like Ruth Dreifuss, the first female President in 1999).
*   **Resignation:** She resigned in 1989 following a political scandal involving her husband, but her role as a pioneer remains a key fact in Swiss political history.
