# ⚖️ Prohibited Actions: Libel and Slander

### The Core Definition
The correct answer is **(a)**: In Switzerland, it is forbidden to **publicly spread false claims about a person** (libel/slander).

### Protecting Reputation
While freedom of expression is a fundamental right, it ends where it violates the **personal honor** of others.
*   **Slander (Verleumdung):** Intentionally spreading lies to damage someone's reputation.
*   **Defamation (Üble Nachrede):** Spreading damaging claims without knowing if they are true.

### What IS allowed:
*   **Criticizing the Government (b):** You are allowed to publicly disagree with the Federal Council or any politician. This is a core part of democracy.
*   **Divorce (c):** Marriage is a contract that can be legally dissolved.
*   **Demonstrations (d):** Peaceful assembly and protest are fundamental rights (as long as they are registered with the local authorities).
