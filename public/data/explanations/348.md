### A Piece of Madagascar in Zurich
Opened in 2003, the Masoala Hall is a unique conservation project.
*   **Dimensions:** It is a massive glass hall covering 11,000 square meters, reaching heights of up to 30 meters.
*   **Environment:** Inside, the tropical climate of Madagascar is replicated. It contains over 500 plant species and around 50 animal species (including lemurs, flying foxes, and exotic birds) roaming freely.
*   **Conservation:** The Zoo Zurich uses part of the hall's revenue to fund the protection of the actual Masoala National Park in Madagascar.
*   **Experience:** For visitors, it is an immersive experience where they can walk on a treetop path and feel as if they have traveled thousands of kilometers to the Indian Ocean.

It is one of the most famous and popular attractions in the City of Zurich, attracting over a million visitors each year.
